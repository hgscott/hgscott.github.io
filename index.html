Hello! I’m Rosalie.

I use illustration to honor and celebrate the places and creatures that often go unnoticed. My goal is to remind viewers of the amazing intricacy of our planet in an attempt to encourage environmental stewardship.

Recently, I was honored to be the Artist-in-Residence at Great Smoky Mountains National Park and with the National Audubon Society. My clients include the Smithsonian and the U.S. Fish & Wildlife Service. This year, I’m really honored to be the 2022 Eckelberry Fellow, sponsored by the Drexel University Academy of Natural Sciences.

I am also an educator. So far, I’ve enjoyed teaching watercolor & outdoor observation workshops in schools, national parks and REI stores. I’ve also taught over 50,000 students as a Top Teacher at Skillshare and all of my classes are also available individually on my website. My first book, Watercolor in Nature: Paint Woodland Wildlife and Botanicals with 20 Beginner-Friendly Projects, was published by Page Street Publishing Co. in 2021.

Stay up to date with all my artsy adventures on Instagram @rosaliehaizlett
